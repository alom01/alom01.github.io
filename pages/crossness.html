Crossness Pumping Station

Abbey Wood, London SE2 9AQ

About Crossness Pumping Station

Described as the 'Cathedral of Sewage', the Crossness Pumping Station was built by Sir Joseph Bazalgette as part of Victorian London's very much needed main sewerage system. It was officially opened by the Prince of Wales in April 1865. The Beam Engine House is a Grade I Listed Industrial Building built in the Romanesque style and features some of the most amazing pretty Victorian cast ironwork to be found today. It also contains the four original pumping engines, which are possibly the largest remaining rotative beam engines in the world. 150 years old, 1,000 tons of cutting edge Victorian engineering hailed in its day as a modern wonder of science and engineering

Unfortunately, due to asbestos being found in the Beam Engine House in 2017, the pumping station has been temporarily closed to the public. However, it will re-open in March 2019.
